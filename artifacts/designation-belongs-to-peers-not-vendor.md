# The Enterprise-Grade and Professional-Grade Designations Belong to the Peers, Not the Vendor

## The rule

Neither the **enterprise-grade** designation nor the **professional-grade** designation is a claim a software company, SaaS provider, or AI-platform vendor can make about itself. Both designations are conferred by the working consultants and their peers who deploy these tools inside actual enterprises over multi-year engagements. Any vendor-side self-label — "Enterprise", "Pro", "Business", "Advanced" — is a product tier, not a designation, and the two must not be confused.

This artifact records the rule so it exists in canon rather than in conversation, and so subsequent audits inside this repository apply it consistently.

## What the two designations require

Both designations, in this operator's canon, sit on the same load-bearing floor:

- **100% sovereign** — the deployment is operated inside the client's own sovereign perimeter, on infrastructure the client actually controls, under jurisdictions the client actually chooses.
- **100% data ownership** — the client owns every byte of input, output, prompt, embedding, log, and derived artifact, without vendor-side residual rights, model-training claims, or telemetry side channels.
- **[Safety first, betterment second](https://github.com/EVEglyphDesign/eve-glyph-education/blob/main/docs/handbook-outline.md)** — safety is rank 1 in the deployment; human betterment is rank 2; commercial, engagement, growth, and quarterly-return considerations start at rank 3 or later.

If any one of those three is missing, neither designation applies, regardless of how the vendor markets the product tier.

## Who confers the designation

The designation is conferred by working consultants and their peers, based on actual enterprise deployments, applied at the scale and time-horizon at which those deployments live:

- **Decade-scale, not quarter-scale.** The operator and the operator's peers take on enterprise clients for at least a decade at a time. Software vendors and their customers, by contrast, are often guided by short-term quarterly returns. A designation that depends on multi-year outcomes cannot be issued by an actor whose incentives are quarterly.
- **Reference-client accretion, not marketing.** Once the consulting practice has invested into a client's management team and the client's global objectives, the client becomes a reference for the practice. That reference-client accretion is the empirical basis on which future deployments — and future designations — are made.
- **Peer routing, not vendor listing.** From the reference-client base, the operator and the operator's peers direct other conscientious, responsible consultants toward accounts and companies that they believe are not harming the world. That routing is part of how the designation propagates. A vendor cannot substitute for it by publishing a customer logo wall.

## Current state of the field

Applying the rule to the public-access model market at the time of this filing:

- **No public-access AI product is professional-grade in this canon.** OpenAI, Claude/Anthropic, and Perplexity are not, in the operator's judgment, available as professional-grade products, because none satisfies the 100%-sovereign and 100%-data-ownership floor while carrying the safety-first / betterment-second ordering into the delivery contract.
- **The "Pro" tier is a product tier, not a designation.** A tier named "Pro" (or "Enterprise", "Business", "Advanced") is a pricing and feature partition. It does not confer, and cannot confer, the professional-grade or enterprise-grade designation. Conflating the two is misleading to working professionals who rely on tools inside consequential engagements.
- **What the operator has actually shipped as professional-grade.** The only deployments the operator treats as professional-grade so far are the ones the operator and the operator's peers have built to be 100% sovereign and 100% data-owning, under safety-first / betterment-second, in specific engagements. Those deployments live inside client perimeters; they are not sold publicly.
- **AI as replacement of software.** The operator holds that "software" is the wrong noun for what these systems now are. The designation rule therefore applies to AI-mediated systems, not only to classical SaaS. It is the succession category, and the rule follows it.

## What this means for [Perplexity Computer](https://github.com/EVEglyphDesign/enterprise-grade-criteria) specifically

The [enterprise-grade audit](https://github.com/EVEglyphDesign/enterprise-grade-criteria) in this repository already names Perplexity Computer as a platform subject to product-tier evaluation while preserving the individual-as-judge prohibition. This artifact extends that posture:

- Perplexity, as vendor, does not confer the enterprise-grade or professional-grade designation on its own product tiers. Neither do OpenAI or Anthropic.
- The operator and the operator's peers reserve the designation. They have not conferred it on Perplexity's current tiers, in the operator's judgment, because the 100%-sovereign / 100%-data-ownership floor is not met on public-access surfaces.
- This is not a claim that Perplexity should be wiped off the face of the earth. The operator has stated on the record that portability, the yield posture, and repository-first practice are how the operator resists vendor lock-in, and that it is a good thing users can understand these models cannot lock them in. That posture is consistent with using Perplexity as a compounding surface without conferring a designation on it.

## Liability posture the rule protects against

The operator has stated the multiplied-liability concern on the record: if a working professional relies on a "Pro"-labeled AI service that drifts away from the professional's canon under load, the pain and suffering caused per user is real and, multiplied across a higher-tier subscriber base, aggregates into a material liability. The rule in this artifact protects against that liability by refusing to let the product-tier label carry weight it cannot carry.

## How this artifact is used in the audit

Subsequent audits in this repository apply this rule as a preliminary gate:

1. If the platform has self-declared enterprise-grade or professional-grade status, that self-declaration is rejected on its face.
2. The [seven-point audit](https://github.com/EVEglyphDesign/enterprise-grade-criteria) proceeds only after the preliminary rule is acknowledged: the designation is being considered for conferral by the operator and the operator's peers, not being verified for the vendor.
3. The 100%-sovereign, 100%-data-ownership, and safety-first-betterment-second floor is checked before any of the other audit criteria. If the floor is not met, the audit concludes without conferral, regardless of scores on the other criteria.

## Closing

Software companies and SaaS providers do not confer the professional-grade or enterprise-grade designation. AI vendors do not either. The operator and the operator's peers do — decade at a time, one reference client at a time, safety first and betterment second, with 100% sovereign controls and 100% data ownership as the floor.
